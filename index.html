Jewish Mythology

Exploring the rich mythological traditions from the Torah and Tanakh

Torah Mythology

Creation (Bereishit)

The account of God creating the heavens, earth, and all life in six days, culminating in the creation of Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden.

The Nephilim

Mysterious beings mentioned in Genesis 6:4, often interpreted as giants born from the union of "sons of God" and "daughters of men."

The Flood (Mabul)

The great deluge sent to cleanse the world of corruption, with Noah and his family preserved on the ark along with pairs of every creature.

Tower of Babel

The story of humanity's attempt to build a tower to the heavens, resulting in the confusion of languages and scattering of peoples.

The Burning Bush

Moses encounters a bush that burns but is not consumed, where God reveals His name and commissions Moses to free the Israelites.

Splitting of the Sea

The miraculous parting of the Sea of Reeds (Yam Suf), allowing the Israelites to escape Egypt while the waters consumed Pharaoh's army.

Tanakh Mythology

Leviathan

A massive sea serpent or dragon mentioned in Job, Psalms, and Isaiah, representing primordial chaos that God conquered.

Behemoth

A colossal land creature described in Job 40, often paired with Leviathan as representations of God's power over creation.

The Witch of Endor

A medium consulted by King Saul to summon the spirit of the prophet Samuel, revealing the boundaries between life and death.

Elijah's Ascent

The prophet Elijah being taken to heaven in a chariot of fire and whirlwind, never experiencing death.

Ezekiel's Vision

The prophet's mystical vision of the divine chariot (Merkavah) with its four-faced creatures and wheels within wheels.

The Golem

While later developed in medieval texts, the concept originates from Psalm 139:16, referring to an unformed substance given life.

Mythological Creatures

Cherubim

Winged celestial beings who guard the Garden of Eden and appear atop the Ark of the Covenant, distinct from later artistic depictions.

Seraphim

Six-winged beings surrounding God's throne in Isaiah's vision, crying "Holy, holy, holy" and purifying the prophet with burning coal.

Ophanim

The "wheels" in Ezekiel's vision, described as wheels within wheels covered with eyes, part of the divine chariot.

Rahab

A sea monster representing chaos, mentioned in Job, Psalms, and Isaiah as a creature defeated by God.

Tannin

Sea dragons or serpents mentioned throughout the Tanakh, sometimes associated with Egypt and its Pharaoh.

Shedim

Demonic spirits mentioned in Deuteronomy and Psalms, associated with foreign idol worship and sacrifice.

Key Figures

Adam & Eve

The first humans, created in God's image, whose choices in the Garden of Eden shaped the destiny of humanity.

Enoch

A righteous man who "walked with God" and was taken without experiencing death, inspiring rich mystical traditions.

Samson

A Nazirite judge granted supernatural strength, whose story involves riddles, lions, and the secret of his power in his hair.

The Angel of Death

Referenced in the Passover narrative and elsewhere, this figure carries out divine judgments against the living.

Satan (The Adversary)

In Job and Zechariah, a member of the divine council who tests and accuses humanity before God.

Lilith

Mentioned once in Isaiah 34:14, later traditions expanded her into Adam's first wife and a night demon.
